UHS Handbook & Guidance

Operational Standard v2.0

Purpose: Outlines operational standards, professional expectations, and clinical procedures for all staff working within the UHS Ambulance and Hospital Service to ensure safe, effective, and professional care.

Scope: Applies to all UHS personnel, including Student Paramedics, Emergency Care Assistants/Technicians, Paramedics, Senior Paramedics, Critical Care/HEMS, HARTS, Nurses, Doctors, Hospital-based clinical staff, and Supervisors/Command leads.

All personnel must maintain a clean, professional appearance at all times.

3.1 General

  • Uniform must be clean, well-fitted, and appropriate to the role.
  • High-visibility jackets must be worn at roadside, outdoor, or hazardous incidents.
  • Footwear must be black, enclosed, and suitable for emergencies.
  • ID badges visible at all times.
  • PPE used appropriately. No unauthorised headgear or non-issued clothing.

3.2 Role-Specific

  • Ambulance: Green operational uniform, hi-vis for outdoors, stab vest where issued, clinical PPE.
  • HEMS: Aviation-approved flight suit, specialist PPE.
  • HARTS: Technical uniform, BA equipment when required, specialist PPE (CBRN, water, confined spaces), helmets, harnesses.
  • Hospital: Scrubs/tunics, appropriate PPE, hair tied back, no loose jewellery.

4.1 General Rules

Use for intended purpose only. Maintain competency. Report faults immediately. Replenish medical bags after every incident.

4.2 Clinical Equipment

  • Defibrillators: Cardiac arrest or clinically indicated arrhythmias only.
  • Airway & Monitoring: Use according to condition/competency; apply monitoring to unresponsive/unwell patients.
  • Immobilisation: Scoop stretchers/spinal boards for trauma.
  • Controlled Drugs: Handled per legislation and policy.

4.3 Vehicle Equipment

Radios must be used professionally. Blue-lights activated only as required. Check medical equipment at shift start.

5.1 Standard Road Driving

Follow all road traffic laws, speed limits, and drive with consideration. Park safely without obstructing access.

5.2 Emergency Driving

Blue-light response ONLY for: Immediately life-threatening emergencies, urgent inter-facility transfers, major incidents, and rapid deployments (HARTS, cardiac arrest, serious trauma).

Principles: Maintain full control, slow down at junctions/red lights, avoid collisions, use appropriate siren tone, and prioritize public/crew safety.

6.1 Staff Safety

Wear PPE based on risk assessment. Remove yourself from unsafe scenes until supported. Maintain situational awareness.

6.2 Patient Safety

Assess scenes before approaching. Use safe lifting. Secure patients during transport. Communicate before procedures.

6.3 Workplace Safety

Report hazards/faults. Maintain cleanliness. Do not obstruct exits/equipment.

HARTS responds to CBRN/HAZMAT, water rescue, confined spaces, major incidents, and structural collapse.

Equipment: BA used only by trained staff. Carry out dynamic risk assessments before entering hazardous zones.

Team Safety: Operate in pairs/teams, maintain radio contact, follow command structure during major incidents.

8. Conduct & Attitude

Treat patients respectfully, remain calm, avoid conflict, and uphold confidentiality. Use clear radio comms and accurate SBAR handovers. Follow national guidelines and participate in training.

9. Supervision & Discipline

Leaders promote safe practice; staff are accountable for actions. Breaches may result in: Verbal advice, written warnings, temporary suspension, removal from duties, or review of professional status.

General Hospital Policies

A hospital lockdown may only be initiated by the Police Department (PD). No member of UHS staff has the authority to order this.

If initiated, it must be documented and reported to GOLD COMMAND. A joint decision between UHS Gold Command and PD enacts it based on risk.

Continuity of Care: Triage tents must be established without delay. Treat members of the public within tents. No staff should remain idle; focus on patient care and public safety.

Complaints

All complaints are confidential. File via the UHS Internal Complaints Form.

  • C (Catastrophic Haemorrhage): Direct pressure, field dressing, Celox, Tourniquet, TXA.
  • A (Airway): Patent/Clear/Occluded. Jaw lift/chin thrust. Use adjuncts.
  • B (Breathing): Rate and effort. Auscultation. SpO2. Consider oxygen.
  • C (Circulation): Skin colour/cap refill. Pulse rate/quality. BP.
  • D (Disability): BM, AVPU, GCS, PERRLa.
  • E (Expose): Undress to check injuries. Cover up ASAP to prevent hypothermia.

Airways

Head Tilt-Chin Lift: Standard method.

Jaw Thrust: Use for suspected spinal injuries.

OPA (Oropharyngeal): "Invert, Insert, Rotate, Locate." Use on unconscious patients.

NPA (Nasopharyngeal): Insert in nostril, bevel toward septum.

CPR / BLS

Start if unconscious and not breathing. 30 compressions : 2 breaths. Average 2 compressions/second. Connect Defib/AED and shock if advised. Repeat for 3 sets. If no ROSC after 10 minutes, reassess.

DNR (Do Not Resuscitate)

Valid for chronic illnesses, terminal age. NOT valid for acts of violence (stabbing) or young healthy individuals (Police/Fire).

Heart Attack (MI): Chest pain, sweating, left arm pain. Give 300mg Aspirin, GTN (if BP safe), O2.

Anaphylaxis: Swelling, wheeze, low BP. Give IM Adrenaline (0.5mg), high-flow O2.

Asthma Attack: Wheeze, short of breath. Give Salbutamol (Neb/MDI), O2.

Hypoglycaemia: Confusion, sweating, collapse. Give Oral Glucose if awake.

Opioid Overdose: Slow/absent breathing, pinpoint pupils. Give Naloxone, ventilate.

Transport Immediately If: Chest pain, Major Trauma, Anaphylaxis, Severe Asthma, Stroke, Unconscious, Serious bleeding, Seizure > 5 mins.

Major Bleeding: Direct pressure, bandage, tourniquet.

Head Injury: Keep airway clear, minimise movement, X-ray/MRI needed.

Spinal Precautions: Use if high-energy mechanism, neck/back pain, unconscious. MRI required.

Blue-lights should only be used for: Life-threatening emergencies, urgent transfers, major incidents, and rapid deployments.

Exemptions allowed under Emergency Conditions:

  • Exceeding speed limits (See warning below)
  • Travelling on the wrong side of the road
  • Treating RED lights as give way
  • Stopping on a motorway
Speed Rule: Driving at a speed exceeding 20 mph above the speed limit is NOT recommended. Breaching this may result in loss of CERAD certification.

Q: Primary 360 Lights

K: Rear Red emergency lights

Y: Additional lighting

G: Toggle Sirens

R: Cycle Siren Tones

E: Bullhorn

Siren Tones

  • Wail: Normal siren, for high-speed roads to warn traffic far ahead.
  • Yelp: Short range, use when approaching junctions.
  • Phaser: Very loud, use crossing busy intersections. Short durations only.
  • Bull-Horn: Additional warning in heavy traffic. Do not use aggressively.

Motorway Parking

  • Flowing Traffic: Approach in lanes 2/3 with ALL equipment on.
  • Slow Traffic: Approach on hard shoulder with visual warnings only.
  • Stationary Traffic: Approach on hard shoulder with ALL equipment on.

Tandem Driving (Convoy)

Keep a reasonable distance. Utilize DIFFERENT siren tones than the lead vehicle. If transporting to ED, remain behind the box ambulance at all times.

Lane Discipline

Remain in lane three (closest to central reservation) under blue lights. DO NOT mount pavements to get around vehicles unless entering a property. If stuck at a red light with no safe way around, turn OFF sirens, keep lights ON, and wait.

Blue-lights should only be used for: Life-threatening emergencies, urgent transfers, major incidents, and rapid deployments.

Exemptions allowed under Emergency Conditions:

  • Exceeding speed limits (See warning below)
  • Travelling on the wrong side of the road
  • Treating RED lights as give way
  • Stopping on a motorway
Speed Rule: Driving at a speed exceeding 20 mph above the speed limit is NOT recommended. Breaching this may result in loss of CERAD certification.

Siren Tones

  • Wail: Normal siren, for high-speed roads to warn traffic far ahead.
  • Yelp: Short range, use when approaching junctions.
  • Phaser: Very loud, use crossing busy intersections. Short durations only.
  • Bull-Horn: Additional warning in heavy traffic. Do not use aggressively.

Motorway Parking

  • Flowing Traffic: Approach in lanes 2/3 with ALL equipment on.
  • Slow Traffic: Approach on hard shoulder with visual warnings only.
  • Stationary Traffic: Approach on hard shoulder with ALL equipment on.

Tandem Driving (Convoy)

Keep a reasonable distance. Utilize DIFFERENT siren tones than the lead vehicle. If transporting to ED, remain behind the box ambulance at all times.

Lane Discipline

Remain in lane three (closest to central reservation) under blue lights. DO NOT mount pavements to get around vehicles unless entering a property. If stuck at a red light with no safe way around, turn OFF sirens, keep lights ON, and wait.
